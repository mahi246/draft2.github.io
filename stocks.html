Stocks

What are stocks?

A stock is defined as a share of ownership in a specific company.The benefits of investing in stocks include: capital appreciation
(when stocks prices rise), dividend payments(when companies distribute earnings to stockholders), and stocks also give the investor an
influence in voting for changes in the company. However, a downside is that there is no guarantee that the stocks you invest in will
increase in price, meaning you may lose money. Likewise, stocks tend to be a long-term investment and therefore you may not receive
a significant return on your investment until a long period of time. However, whether or not you make a profit depends on what price you
buy the stocks at and what price you sell them.

Types of Stocks

Growth Stocks Income Stocks
Growth stocks are usually higher earning than other stocks. They are commonly bought in the hope of capital appreciation and rarely earn dividends. Income stocks have the majority of their earnings through dividends. This type of stock provides investors with a consistent stream of income and is a company that is in mostly stable industries. Some examples include, technology, utility and finance.
Value Stocks Blue-Chip Stocks
Value stocks are regarded as being under-valued but those who invest in them see potential. They have a low ratio of price to earning. These stocks are cheaper to buy but have a high PE(potential earning). Investors buy these stocks in the hope that the company will grow or the industry value will increase. Blue-chip stocks are categorized as stocks in large and stable companies, however they do not grow much. Though blue-chip stocks are expensive, they tend to be low-risk and usually pay dividends.

Start Investing

Before starting to invest in stocks, it is important to research the up and downs of the stock market.
Consider what companies may do well in the future, and remember to diversify your portfolio with stocks
from different industries. Diversifying your portfolio will help lower the risk of losing your investments.
Then open an investing account, which can be done through many online services. As a beginner, start investing
with a little money and as you begin to get more comfortable start investing more money.
